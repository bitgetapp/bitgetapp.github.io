<details style="margin-bottom: 16px; border: 1px solid #ccc; border-radius: 8px;">
<summary><strong>Step 5: Advanced Risk Management & Portfolio Hygiene</strong></strong></summary>

Surviving in futures is about risk management, not prediction.

1.  **Position Sizing:** Never risk more than 1-2% of your total capital on a single trade. This prevents any one loss from being catastrophic.
2.  **Isolated vs. Cross Margin:**
    *   **Isolated Margin:** The margin is allocated to one position only. If that position is liquidated, you lose only the allocated margin. **Safer for beginners.**
    *   **Cross Margin:** Your entire futures balance acts as margin for all positions. It can prevent liquidation on one trade but risks your entire portfolio if multiple trades go wrong.
3.  **Monitor Funding Rates:** Perpetual contracts have periodic funding rates. A high positive rate means longs pay shorts. Factor this into your holding costs.
4.  **Regular Withdrawals:** Periodically withdraw profits to your cold wallet. Do not let all your capital sit on an exchange indefinitely.
