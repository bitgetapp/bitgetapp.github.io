SMS Authentication acts as a fundamental barrier. It's required for logins from new devices and sensitive operations.
1.  In [Security], find "SMS Authentication" and click [Enable].
2.  Select your country/region and enter your **personal, active mobile number**. This number should be under your direct control.
3.  You will receive a 6-digit code via SMS. Enter it promptly on the verification page.
4.  **Critical Backup:** Binance will provide you with a "Backup Key" for SMS. **Write this down on paper and store it in a safe place (not digitally!).** This key is essential if you lose access to your phone number.

**⚠️ Risk Note:** SIM-swap attacks are a real threat. While SMS is good, it should not be your only line of defense. It's a foundational layer, not the ceiling.

<summary style="cursor: pointer; padding: 12px; background-color: #dbeafe; font-weight: bold;">Step 3: Binding Google Authenticator (Your Core Shield)</summary>
<div style="padding: 16px; background-color: white; border-top: 1px solid #93c5fd;">

This is the heart of your security. Google Authenticator (or similar apps like Authy) generates time-based one-time passwords (TOTP) that are immune to SIM-swaps.
1.  In [Security], find "Google Authentication" and click [Enable].
2.  **Download the App:** If you haven't already, install "Google Authenticator" from your phone's official app store.
3.  **Scan the QR Code:** Binance will display a QR code. Open Google Authenticator, tap "+", and select "Scan a QR code." Point your camera at the screen.
4.  **Manual Backup (NON-NEGOTIABLE):** After scanning, Binance will show a 16-digit "Manual Backup Key." **THIS IS THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT PIECE OF INFORMATION IN THIS GUIDE.**
    *   Write it down on paper.
    *   Store it in a physical safe or safety deposit box.
    *   **Do not save it in a note on your phone, computer, or email.**
5.  Enter the 6-digit code generated by the Authenticator app to complete binding.

Now, let's add proactive defenses that control outflow and identify legitimacy.
1.  **Withdrawal Address Whitelist:** Go to [Security] > [Withdrawal Address Management]. Enable "Whitelist."
    *   This feature ensures crypto can only be withdrawn to addresses you have pre-approved and verified.
    *   Adding a new address requires email confirmation and a delay (e.g., 24 hours), thwarting hackers even if they get temporary access.
2.  **Anti-Phishing Code:** In [Security], find this option.
    *   Set a unique word or code (e.g., "CryptoVault2026").
    *   Every official email from Binance will contain this code. If an email lacks it, it's a phishing attempt. **Delete it immediately.**

**Investment Logic:** These settings are like having a time-lock on your safe and a secret handshake. They don't just protect; they give you time and clarity to react.
