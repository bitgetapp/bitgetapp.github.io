### **Why This Isn't Just a Formality: The Underlying Logic**

Binding your phone number is the cornerstone of a security strategy known as "defense in depth." Your password is one layer. Your phone adds a second, physical layer tied to something you possess. It's required for critical actions:

*   **Withdrawals:** Most exchanges mandate phone verification for any asset withdrawal.
*   **Password Resets:** The primary recovery method if you forget your password.
*   **New Device Logins:** Alerts and codes are sent when logging in from an unrecognized browser or device.
*   **Security Alerting:** Immediate SMS notifications for suspicious activity.

> ⚠️ **Risk Alert**
> <div style="background:#7f1a1a; color:#fecaca; padding:12px; border-left:4px solid #dc2626; border-radius:4px;">
> <strong>SIM Swap Attack Awareness:</strong> While phone binding is crucial, it's not invincible. A sophisticated attack called a "SIM swap" can redirect your SMS codes to a hacker's device. This is precisely why enabling app-based 2FA (Google Authenticator) is the **mandatory next step**. It decouples your primary security from the telecom network, making your account exponentially harder to breach.
