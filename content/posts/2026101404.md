## 💡 Why Understanding Maker/Taker Fees is Your First Step to Profit

Before you place your first trade, you must understand how trading fees work. On OKX and most major exchanges, fees are split into two types: **Maker** and **Taker**. This isn't just jargon—it's the key to managing your trading costs effectively.

*   **Maker Orders:** You place an order that **adds liquidity** to the order book (e.g., a limit buy order below the current market price or a limit sell order above it). Your order sits and waits for someone else to match it. For providing this liquidity, you are rewarded with a lower fee.
*   **Taker Orders:** You place an order that **removes liquidity** by immediately matching an existing order on the book (e.g., a market buy/sell or a limit order that matches an existing price). For taking liquidity, you pay a slightly higher fee.

**Why does this matter for a beginner?** Your trading strategy directly impacts your fees. If you're patient and use limit orders strategically, you can trade as a **Maker** and save significantly on fees over hundreds of trades. This is where OKX's rebate program becomes a game-changer.
