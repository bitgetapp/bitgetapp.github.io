### Investment Logic & Case Study Breakdown

The core logic of a grid trading bot is "buy low, sell high" within a predefined range. It places a series of limit buy orders below the current price and limit sell orders above it. As price oscillates, it automatically executes these orders, profiting from volatility.

**Case Study: Trading ETH/USDT in a Range**
*   **Scenario:** ETH is consolidating between $3,200 and $3,600.
*   **Bot Setup:** Price Range: $3,200 - $3,600. Grids: 40. Investment: $2,000 USDT.
*   **Execution:** The bot places 40 buy orders between $3,200-$3,400 and 40 sell orders between $3,400-$3,600. Each time ETH price moves up and down, it buys a slice at a lower grid and sells a slice at a higher grid.
*   **Outcome:** Even if ETH's price ends where it started, the bot generates profit from the micro-fluctuations. The total profit is the sum of all individual grid profits minus trading fees.
