### **Your Step-by-Step Fee Optimization Blueprint**

**1. Decode the Fee Structure: Maker vs. Taker**
Bitget, like most major exchanges, uses a maker-taker model. The **maker fee** is charged when you add liquidity to the order book (e.g., placing a limit order that isn't immediately filled). The **taker fee** is charged when you remove liquidity (e.g., placing a market order or a limit order that fills instantly). As of 2026, Bitget's standard spot trading fee is **0.1% for takers**. However, makers often enjoy a rebate or a significantly lower fee, sometimes even **negative fees** (you get paid to provide liquidity) for high-volume traders.

**2. Secure Your VIP Status & Fee Tiers**
Your trading volume over a 30-day period (in BTC or USDT terms) determines your VIP level. Higher tiers offer progressively lower fees. Check your current level in the "Fee Rate" section of your account. The key is **consolidating your volume**. Avoid splitting trades across multiple exchanges if your goal is to climb Bitget's VIP ladder for the best rates.

**4. Master the Copy Trading Fee Mechanics**
Bitget's popular copy trading feature has its own fee model. For followers, the strategy is to choose lead traders with a consistent, long-term profit history rather than chasing high-risk, high-fee "stars."

**5. Leverage Futures & Derivatives Wisely**

Before entering a perpetual swap trade, always check the funding rate history. Trading against a consistently high funding rate can erode profits faster than the trading fee itself.
