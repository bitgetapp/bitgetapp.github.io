#### **Step-by-Step Guide**

**1.  Eligibility & Staking Phase**
Before any rewards are calculated, you must become an eligible node. This starts on the OKX Earn platform. Navigate to "Staking" or "Node Programs" and select the specific node offering you wish to join (e.g., OKB Node, ETH Node). You will need to lock up the required amount of the designated asset (like OKB) for the specified staking period. Your node status and reward eligibility begin immediately upon successful staking.

**2.  The Reward Accumulation & Calculation Window**
This is the critical period most users misunderstand. Rewards are **not** distributed in real-time. OKX typically uses a **daily snapshot** mechanism. The system takes a snapshot of your staked balance and the network's total staked amount at a specific time each day (e.g., 00:00 UTC). Your share of that day's reward pool is calculated based on your proportion in this snapshot. This calculated reward accrues in the background but is not yet in your available balance.

**3.  The Settlement & Distribution Cycle**
Here's the key: the *calculation* is daily, but the *distribution* follows a set cycle. For most OKX node programs, the **settlement and distribution occur once per week**. All the daily accrued rewards from the previous 7 days are tallied up and then distributed to your funding account or staking sub-account. You will usually see this happen on a specific weekday. Mark your calendar for this weekly "payday."

**4.  Querying Your Rewards (The How-To)**
You don't have to guess. To check your accrued and historical rewards:
*   Go to **OKX App/Website > Earn > Staking**.
*   Find your active node staking position and click "Details" or "Manage."
*   Look for tabs like **"Reward History"** or **"Earnings Record."** This page will show a detailed log of every distribution, including date, amount, and status.
*   For pending (accrued but not yet distributed) rewards, check the **"Estimated Rewards"** or **"Accrued"** field on the main staking page.

**5.  Claiming & Reinvestment**
Once distributed, rewards are typically automatically credited to your funding account. Some programs may require a manual "Claim" click. Your strategic decision begins here: withdraw for profit or compound by re-staking the rewards to increase your node share and future earnings.

#### **Pro Tips & Critical Risk Warnings**

**> Pro Tip: The Snapshot Timing**
Find out the exact UTC time of the daily snapshot. Ensure your funds are staked **before** this time to be included in that day's calculation. Adding funds right after the snapshot means you miss a full day of accrual.

**> Risk Warning #1: Impermanent Lock-up**
Node staking involves locking your assets. You cannot trade or withdraw them during the staking period. Market volatility during this lock-up period represents a significant opportunity cost risk. Your capital is illiquid.

**> Pro Tip: Audit Your Weekly Statement**
Don't just glance at the balance. Each week, manually verify the distributed amount against your estimated share. Use the formula: `(Your Staked Amount / Total Network Staked) * Weekly Reward Pool`. This ensures the system is working as promised and builds your analytical skill.

**> Risk Warning #2: Protocol Parameter Changes**
The exchange can alter key parameters: the reward pool size, distribution frequency, or even the required staking amount. These changes can directly and negatively impact your projected APR. Always read official announcements related to node programs.

**> Pro Tip: Leverage the Explorer**
For transparency-focused nodes (like certain ETH nodes), you can cross-reference your rewards using the relevant blockchain explorer (Etherscan) by checking the staking contract address. This provides an immutable, third-party verification.

**> Risk Warning #3: Platform & Smart Contract Risk**
You are ultimately trusting OKX's infrastructure and, if applicable, the underlying smart contracts. While low, risks of technical glitches, exploits, or extreme black swan events at the platform level cannot be entirely eliminated. Never stake more than you can afford to lose.
