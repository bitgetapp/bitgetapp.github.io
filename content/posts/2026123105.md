### The 2026 Complete Troubleshooting & Fix Guide for OKX KYC Failure

**1.  The Pre-Check: Document Preparation is 80% of the Battle**
Before you even submit, get your documents camera-ready. This is the most common failure point.
*   **Accepted Documents:** A valid, government-issued passport, national ID card, or driver's license. **Passports are universally the smoothest option.**
*   **Photo Quality Rules:**
    *   **Clarity:** High resolution, no blur, no glare. Every letter and security feature must be crystal clear.
    *   **Completeness:** All four corners of the document must be visible. Do not crop out any part.
    *   **Environment:** Take the photo on a dark, contrasting, non-reflective surface. Ensure even, bright lighting (natural light is best) with no shadows falling on the document.
    *   **Authenticity:** Never edit, filter, or alter the document image. The system detects digital tampering instantly.

**2.  The Submission Process: A Frame-by-Frame Breakdown**
The live capture process is where user error creeps in.
*   **Face Scan / Liveness Detection:**
    *   Follow the on-screen prompts *exactly*. If it says "Blink," blink naturally. If it says "Turn head," do so slowly.
    *   Ensure your face is fully within the frame, with good front-facing lighting. Avoid backlighting from windows.
    *   Remove hats, glasses, or anything obscuring your face. A simple, clear shot is key.
*   **Document Scan:**
    *   Use the in-app camera. Do not upload a pre-taken photo from your gallery unless specifically prompted for a "file upload" option.
    *   Hold your phone steady. Let the auto-capture function work, or manually tap the shutter when the document is perfectly aligned and in focus.
    *   For ID cards, you will typically need to capture both the front and back. Don't rush the second side.

**3.  Information Accuracy: The Devil is in the Details**
The data you type must be a perfect, letter-for-letter match with your document.
*   **Name:** Enter your **full legal name** exactly as it appears on your passport or ID. If your document shows "Last Name, First Name," follow that order. Middle names matter.
*   **Date of Birth:** Double-check the day, month, and year. A transposed number is a guaranteed fail.
*   **Document Number:** This is case-sensitive and includes all letters and digits. "ABC123456" is not the same as "abc123456".
*   **Address:** Use a consistent, verifiable residential address. **Risk Warning:** Do NOT use a PO Box or virtual office address. This is a major red flag for compliance systems and will lead to rejection or future account restrictions.
